The way the internet was meant to be

Central servers are inefficient

With a central server or a CDN, users can only be served a file from a single server at a time, instead of getting pieces from multiple computers simultaneously. As server are not allocated instantly, server power always lags behind network usage. This results in a waste of ressources, or in contrary, slow performance.

The DataBridge Network makes it possible to distribute high volumes of data with high efficiency and speed. As more users result in better performance, the DataBridge Network scales dynamically and is the most flexible network to this date.

Bandwidth is expensive

With ever more traffic, high-resolution images and 4k video, bandwidth costs are increasingly becoming a barrier for delivering content online. By integrating users as active components of the network, a distributed solution does not rely on bandwidth for most of its content delivery. With video content, such a P2P approach could save 60% in distribution costs.

The DataBridge Network effectively reduces bandwidth costs through distributed content delivery.

Servers are energy-hungry

Energy consumption is expected to become an internet bottleneck in the next years. Central servers consume up to 70% of the total transmission energy and are responsible for the associated carbon dioxide (CO2) and heat emissions. In 2020, communications networks will emit as much CO2 as civil aviation. Reducing the ecological impact of the internet will be one of the major challenges of the next decade.

The DataBridge Network offers an ecologically clean alternative to server access.

Simple integration into your existing architecture

Modern day platforms are growing ever more complicated, combining hosting, multiple content delivery networks and data anlytic services.

The DataBridge Network can be seamlessy added on top of your existing architecture in a few clicks.
